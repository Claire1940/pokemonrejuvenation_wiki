Before diving into the complex **pokemon rejuvenation quests**, you must ensure your team and inventory are prepared. The Type: Null quest is not accessible until you have progressed significantly through the main story, specifically reaching Chapter 5 and obtaining the necessary HMs to navigate the waterlogged sections of the map.

| Requirement Type | Specific Detail | Necessity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Story Progress** | Reach Chapter 5 (Post-Valerie) | Mandatory |

| **Location** | River's End Laboratory | Starting Point |
| **Key Items** | Upgrade and Magnet | Required for Repair |

Accessing River's End requires navigating the long route to Route 2. Once inside the lab, you will meet Conrad, who introduces you to the Professor. The quest officially kicks off once you agree to investigate an inconsistency located beneath the Wispy Tower—the same location where players previously encountered Giratina.

<Callout type="warning" title="Version Differences">
In version 13.5 and later, the Type: Null quest has been streamlined. Previously, you had to find Type: Null in a specific patch of grass, but now the encounter is handled directly through the lab's restoration process.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Type Null Restoration">

The restoration of Type: Null is a multi-stage process involving environmental puzzles and item gathering. Follow these **pokemon rejuvenation quests** steps to ensure you don't miss any critical triggers.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The River's End Interview">
    Visit the lab in River's End and speak with Matthew Vassel. Answer his questions correctly: The season is Summer, and the location of the island is East of Akuwa Town.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Wispy Tower Investigation">
    Travel to the basement (the "bowels") of Wispy Tower. Look for a waterlogged area that was previously inaccessible. You will find a puzzle involving "Shadow red among the old."
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Solving the Anomaly Puzzle">
    Align the pieces in the basement to "undo the fold." This reveals a rusted Pokéball containing an anomaly. This anomaly is the "inconsistency" the Professor is looking for.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Gathering Repair Materials">
    Return to the lab. You need an **Upgrade** (found in Silph Co or as hidden loot) and **Magnet Powder**. Magnet Powder is obtained by grinding down a Magnet item.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Data Transfer">
    Hand the materials to Eisen. He will use the Magnet Powder and Upgrade to transfer the data from the rusted, water-damaged ball to a new, functional one.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Shiny Hunting Tip">
Type: Null is not shiny-locked in this quest. If you wish to obtain a shiny version, save your game right before Eisen finishes the data transfer. In version 13.5, players have reported success within 20-30 minutes of soft resetting.
</Callout>

</Section>
