<Section icon={Building} title="Understanding the Recruitment System in V14">

The ability to recruit and "make your own employee" in Pokemon Rejuvenation is a sophisticated mechanic tied to the expansion of the Aevium region. Unlike standard RPGs where NPCs are static, V14 introduces several layers of interaction where your choices, Karma, and quest completion directly influence the workforce of your home base or associated facilities in Grand Dream City (GDC).

In the current V14 build, this feature is most prominent when managing the various floors of the GDC Department Store or completing the "Help Center" questlines. Players aren't just hiring random NPCs; they are often selecting individuals with specific backstories that can be "customized" through dialogue choices and quest outcomes.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Recruiting Custom Employees">

Recruiting the right staff requires a blend of exploration and social engineering. Follow these steps to ensure you don't miss out on the best "make your own employee" opportunities during your V14 playthrough.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Complete Regional Help Center Tasks">
    Before reaching the major hubs, you must establish a reputation. Complete at least 50% of the Help Center requests in East Gearen and Sheridan Village. This builds your **Karma**, which is a hidden stat required for many NPCs to trust you with their employment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Reach Grand Dream City (Chapter 9)">
    The core "make your own employee" mechanics are gated behind the GDC arc. Once you gain access to the Central District, visit the Department Store. Speak to the manager on the ground floor to initiate the "Staffing Crisis" sidequest.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Locate Potential Candidates">
    Candidates are scattered across Aevium. You will find them in the Night Market, the Judicial District, and even hidden in the Darchlight Woods. Each candidate has a "Trait" (e.g., Charismatic, Diligent, or Resourceful) that determines their effectiveness.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finalize Customization via Dialogue">
    When you find a candidate, your dialogue choices will "set" their role. For example, telling an NPC that "Efficiency is key" might turn them into a discount provider, while "Variety is better" might make them stock rarer items.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Missable Content Warning">
Several recruitment options in Grand Dream City are **time-sensitive**. If you progress the main story to the "GDC Crisis" chapters without finalizing your staff, certain NPCs may become unavailable or move to the Renegade route areas where they cannot be recruited.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Impact of Story Routes on Recruitment">

Your choice between the **Paragon Route** and the **Renegade Route** significantly impacts your ability to "make your own employee." In V14, the story branches at Chapter 16, but the seeds are sown much earlier through your interactions with potential recruits.

- **Paragon Route**: Focuses on community building. You will have access to a wider variety of "Helpful" traits. NPCs recruited on this route often provide services that help with team flexibility and Field Effect management.
- **Renegade Route**: Focuses on power and efficiency. Recruits may be fewer, but they offer "Darker" services, such as access to rare Poison/Dark TMs and items typically restricted to Team Xen facilities.

<Callout type="info" title="Karma Check">
Many high-tier employees require a positive Karma score. If you find an NPC refusing to join your facility, check your Karma at the local psychic or completionist NPC. You may need to complete more "good" sidequests to unlock them.
</Callout>
