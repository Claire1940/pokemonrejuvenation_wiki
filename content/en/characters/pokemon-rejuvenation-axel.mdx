<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Analyzing the Desert Field Mechanics">

The battle against Axel is fought entirely on the **Desert Field**, a unique environment that heavily favors Ground, Rock, and Fire-type Pokémon while actively punishing Water-types. Understanding the passive modifiers of this field is crucial to formulating a winning strategy, as standard type advantages are shifted.

Under the Desert Field, Water-type moves have their damage output halved, meaning your traditional Water-type counters will fail to secure one-hit knockouts. Conversely, Ground and Rock-type moves receive a 1.5x power multiplier, turning Axel's Earthquakes into devastating, team-wiping attacks.

| Move Type / Hazard | Field Modifier Effect |
|---|---|
| **Ground-type Moves** | Power multiplied by 1.5x |
| **Rock-type Moves** | Power multiplied by 1.5x |
| **Water-type Moves** | Power reduced by 50% (Evaporated) |
| **Fire-type Moves** | Power multiplied by 1.2x |
| **Sandstorm Weather** | Boosts Rock-type Special Defense by 1.5x |
| **Arena Hazards** | Spikes and Stealth Rock damage is increased |

To bypass these restrictions, you must either bring Pokémon that naturally benefit from the field modifiers or use specific moves to alter the terrain entirely. Moves like Blizzard, Rain Dance, or Grassy Terrain can temporarily freeze, wash away, or cover the desert floor, neutralizing Axel's environmental advantage.

<Callout type="info" title="Field Transformation">
Using Blizzard or Ice Beam on the Desert Field will not permanently change it, but it will temporarily mitigate the Water-type penalty for a few turns. Utilizing a dedicated terrain-changing move is the most reliable way to strip Axel of his offensive buffs.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Battle Strategy">

Defeating Axel requires a structured approach that prioritizes weather control, hazard removal, and targeted physical defense. Follow this tactical sequence to dismantle his sand synergy and secure the Terra Badge.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Neutralize the Hippowdon Lead">
    Axel always leads with Hippowdon to set up Sandstorm and Stealth Rock. Do not lead with a fragile sweeper. Instead, lead with a bulky Grass-type or a Pokémon equipped with Taunt to prevent him from laying hazards or using Yawn. Knock out Hippowdon as quickly as possible to limit the duration of the weather.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Clear the Sandstorm Weather">
    Once Hippowdon is down, immediately change the weather. Use a Pokémon with Snow Warning (like Abomasnow or Alolan Ninetales) or Drizzle (like Pelipper) to override the Sandstorm. This instantly cuts Excadrill's speed in half, rendering his primary sweeper manageable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Bypass Earthquakes with Levitate">
    Axel's team relies heavily on Ground-type attacks. Keep at least two Flying-type Pokémon or Levitate users in your active rotation. Switching a Levitate user into a predicted Earthquake grants you a free turn to set up screens, use status moves, or initiate a counter-attack.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Dismantle Mega Steelix">
    Mega Steelix is Axel's defensive anchor. It possesses massive physical defense, making physical attacks useless. Save your strongest Special Attackers, such as a Fire-type utilizing the Desert Field's 1.2x boost, to melt through its health bar before it can setup Iron Defense.
  </Step>
</Steps>
