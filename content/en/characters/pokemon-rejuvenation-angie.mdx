<Section icon={Swords} title="Battle Mechanics: The Frozen Dimensional Field">

The most dangerous aspect of fighting **pokemon rejuvenation angie** is the **Frozen Dimensional Field**. This field is not just a cosmetic change; it fundamentally alters move properties and damage calculations. If you go into this fight unprepared, her Ice-type Pokémon will likely one-shot your entire team.

| Move Type / Effect | Modification on Frozen Field |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Ice Moves** | Damage increased by 1.5x |
| **Fire Moves** | Damage decreased by 50% (unless field is melted) |
| **Freeze Chance** | Doubled for all moves with a freeze secondary effect |
| **Nature Power** | Becomes Blizzard |
| **Secret Power** | Has a 30% chance to freeze the target |

To win, you must either embrace the cold or find ways to disrupt it. Moves like **Magma Storm**, **Eruption**, or **Flare Blitz** can temporarily change the field, but Angie's team is designed to reset it quickly. 

<Callout type="success" title="Strategy Success">
Using a Pokémon with the **Thick Fat** ability (like Hariyama or Snorlax) can negate the massive damage boost Angie receives from the field, allowing you to stall out her most dangerous sweepers.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Angie's Team Composition (Chapter 7 vs Chapter 15)">

Angie is battled multiple times throughout the 2026 narrative. Her most famous encounter is at the Battlefield of the Gods, but she returns in Chapter 15 at the **Castillo de la Ángel** with a significantly upgraded roster and a tag-team partner, Cera.

### Battlefield of the Gods (Chapter 7)
In this Double Battle, Angie focuses on sheer power and field synergy. She does not use healing items in the initial version of this fight, but her "Faithful Servant" title reflects her high-level AI.

| Pokémon | Primary Role | Key Threat |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Froslass** | Speed / Utility | Destiny Bond / Cursed Body |

### Castillo de la Ángel (Chapter 15)
This is a Tag Battle alongside Cera. The stakes are higher, and the levels are significantly increased. By this point, Angie has recreated her former servants as ice clones, showcasing her growing madness.

<Callout type="info" title="Important">

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Tactical Tips and Counters">

Defeating **pokemon rejuvenation angie** requires a mix of defensive typing and offensive field manipulation. Because her team relies on the Frozen Dimensional Field, your primary goal should be changing the weather or the field itself.

**Key Strategies for 2026:**
- **Weather Control**: While Hail (Snow) buffs her, bringing a **Drought** or **Sand Stream** user can strip her of her Blizzard accuracy and chip away at her Froslass.
- **Steel Types**: Pokémon like **Metagross** or **Lucario** resist her Ice attacks and can hit back with 4x effective Steel or Fighting moves. However, watch out for her Mamoswine's Earthquake.
- **Entry Hazards**: Setting up **Stealth Rock** is vital. Since almost her entire team is Ice-type, they will take 25% damage every time they switch in.
- **The Magma Stone**: While you only get half of it during the main story, its lore significance highlights that Fire is her ultimate weakness.
