<Section icon={Map} title="Field Effects and Starter Synergies">

Field Effects are the defining mechanic of Pokémon Rejuvenation. Almost every major battle takes place on a unique field that boosts specific types, alters move mechanics, or inflicts passive damage. Choosing a starter that can manipulate, destroy, or abuse these fields is a highly effective strategy for overcoming difficult bosses.

Certain moves can completely shatter fragile fields, resetting the battlefield to a neutral state and stripping the opponent of their home-field advantage.

| Starter | Field-Altering Move | Interaction and Impact |
| Turtwig | Earthquake | Shatters fragile fields (like caves or mirrors), removing enemy buffs |
| Popplio | Hyper Voice | Interacts with acoustic fields, dealing boosted sonic damage |
| Grookey | Grassy Surge | Overwrites hostile terrains with Grassy Terrain, providing passive healing |
| Scorbunny | Pyro Ball | Ignites grassy, forest, or icy fields, causing massive area-of-effect damage |

Understanding these interactions allows you to turn a disadvantageous matchup into a sweep. For example, if an opponent relies on a dark, shadowy field to boost their Ghost-type attacks, using a Fire-type starter to ignite the field can completely disrupt their strategy.

<Callout type="tip" title="Field Control Strategy">
Always carry a Pokémon with a field-changing move. If your starter cannot alter the field directly, ensure your supporting team members have moves like Mist, Blizzard, or Grassy Terrain to neutralize hostile environments.
</Callout>
