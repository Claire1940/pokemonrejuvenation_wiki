<Section icon={Shield} title="pokemon rejuvenation nuzlocke Rules & Core Mechanics">

A **pokemon rejuvenation nuzlocke** is widely considered one of the most demanding fan-game challenges in the entire monster-battling community. Unlike vanilla games, Pokémon Rejuvenation V14 features highly optimized enemy AI, competitive movesets, custom Crest items, and complex Field Effects that can instantly turn a favorable type matchup into a swift team wipe. To survive the journey across the Aevium region, you must establish a clear set of rules and understand how the game's unique mechanics alter traditional Nuzlocke strategies.

Standard Nuzlocke rules apply, but several custom adjustments are highly recommended to balance the game's immense difficulty. Below is a comparison of how to structure your ruleset depending on your preferred level of challenge.

| Rule Type | Standard Nuzlocke | Hardcore Nuzlocke | Rejuvenation V14 Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fainting** | Permanent box/release | Permanent box/release | **Strict release** to avoid temptation |
| **Encounters** | First per route | First per route | Pinwheel clause allowed for static gifts |
| **Level Caps** | Soft cap | Strict cap | **Mandatory** using the `hardcap` password |
| **Items** | Healing items allowed | No items in battle | **Held items only** to match optimized AI |
| **EV Training** | Allowed | Allowed | Highly recommended to match boss scaling |

Using the game's built-in password system is virtually mandatory for a fair run. The developer-sanctioned passwords do not diminish the achievement; instead, they eliminate tedious grinding and ensure that both your team and the opposing bosses compete on a level playing field.

<Callout type="warning" title="Level Cap Danger">
If you do not use the `hardcap` password, accidental overleveling will cause your Pokémon to ignore commands in battle. This mechanical penalty can easily ruin a gym fight and end your entire run prematurely.
</Callout>

Custom Crests are another game-defining mechanic to watch out for. These unique held items provide massive, custom stat boosts and mechanical changes to specific, otherwise underpowered Pokémon. When planning your route encounters, keep an eye out for Crest locations, as they can elevate a low-tier encounter into a top-tier defensive pivot or offensive sweeper.

<Section icon={Target} title="Early-Game Survival & Level Cap Management">

The early hours of a **pokemon rejuvenation nuzlocke**—specifically Chapters 1 through 4—are where the vast majority of runs fail. Your encounter pool is limited, your team lacks advanced coverage options, and early bosses utilize punishing Field Effects that boost their damage output to lethal levels. 

To navigate this critical phase, follow a systematic approach to setting up your run, managing your resources, and preparing for the first major badge challenges.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Password Initialization">
    Enter your chosen passwords during the opening train sequence. Activating `hardcap` prevents accidental overleveling, while `fullivs` guarantees that all wild encounters and gift Pokémon generate with perfect individual values, matching the stats of opposing boss teams.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Starter Selection">
    Choose a starter that offers immediate early-game utility. While all Gen 1-9 starters are viable, those with early access to status moves, high natural bulk, or setup capabilities provide a much higher safety margin during the first three chapters.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Encounter Routing">
    Maximize your encounter pool by visiting every accessible sub-area in East Gearen City and Route 2 before fighting the first Gym Leader. Delaying certain encounters until you have access to better Poké Balls or specific weather conditions can yield much stronger team members.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Field Effect Scanning">
    Before entering any major arena, check the Field Effect tooltip in your game menu. Identify which moves are boosted, which types receive defensive buffs, and whether any of your current team members will take passive damage at the end of each turn.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Managing the strict level caps is essential. Each chapter imposes a hard ceiling on your team's levels, matching the ace Pokémon of the next major boss. Use this breakdown of early-game milestones to plan your training and team compositions accordingly.

Rift Pokémon are particularly dangerous. These corrupted bosses possess massive stat boosts, multiple HP bars, and custom shields that reduce incoming damage. Traditional sweeping strategies rarely work against them; instead, you must rely on status conditions, protection moves, and defensive stalling.
