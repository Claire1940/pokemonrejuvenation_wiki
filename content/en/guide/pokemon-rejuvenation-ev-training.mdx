<Section icon={Map} title="Fundamentals of Pokemon Rejuvenation EV Training">

Understanding **pokemon rejuvenation ev training** is the cornerstone of overcoming the game's notorious difficulty spikes. Effort Values (EVs) are hidden points that directly increase a Pokemon's stats. Unlike experience points which level up the Pokemon, EVs allow for specialized growth, turning a standard attacker into a lethal sweeper or a fragile defender into an immovable wall.

In Pokemon Rejuvenation, every Pokemon can earn a total of **510 EVs**. However, there is a cap of **252 EVs** per individual stat. For every 4 EVs invested in a specific stat, that stat increases by 1 point at level 100. This means a fully trained stat can be up to 63 points higher than an untrained one, which often dictates the outcome of high-stakes gym battles.

| Stat Metric | Maximum Value | Conversion Rate | Total Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Individual Stat** | 252 EVs | 4 EVs = 1 Stat Point | N/A |
| **Total Pokemon Limit** | 510 EVs | 127 Total Stat Points | 510 EVs |
| **Common Spread** | 252 / 252 / 6 | Max 2 Stats + 1 Minor | 510 EVs |

The game makes tracking these values easy. By navigating to the "IVs and EVs" page in the Pokemon Summary, you can see your progress in real-time. The values on the right represent your current EV investment.

<Callout type="info" title="Stat Calculation Tip">
Because 510 is not perfectly divisible by 4, you will always have 2 "wasted" EVs if you max out two stats at 252 (252+252=504). Most players put the remaining 6 points into HP or Speed to ensure every possible point is utilized.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="West Gearen EV Rooms: The Ultimate Farming Method">

The most efficient way to handle **pokemon rejuvenation ev training** is through the dedicated EV Rooms. Located within the **West Gearen City Pokemon Center**, these rooms contain specific wild Pokemon encounters that yield high amounts of EVs for a single stat. However, access is not free; you must prove your worth by earning Achievement Points (AP).

To unlock these rooms, you must visit the **AP Center** located inside the **Chrisola Hotel** in West Gearen. Here, you can exchange your hard-earned AP for Key Cards. Each Key Card corresponds to one of the six primary stats.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Earn Achievement Points">
    Complete side quests, find hidden items, or defeat specific trainers to accumulate AP. Check your trainer card to see your current balance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Purchase Key Cards">
    Head to the Chrisola Hotel in West Gearen. Speak to the AP vendor and buy the Key Cards for the stats you wish to train (e.g., Speed and Attack).
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Access the EV Rooms">
    Return to the West Gearen Pokemon Center. Use the Key Cards on the locked doors in the back to enter the specialized training zones.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Defeat Specialized Pokemon">
    Battle the wild Pokemon inside. Every encounter in these rooms is guaranteed to provide EVs for the stat associated with that room.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Power Items and Traditional Training">

If you haven't reached West Gearen or prefer traditional methods, **pokemon rejuvenation ev training** can be done anywhere wild Pokemon appear. Every Pokemon you defeat yields a specific number of EVs based on its species. For example, defeating a Pidgey usually grants 1 Speed EV, while a Machop grants 1 Attack EV.

To make this viable, you must use **Power Items**. These held items grant a flat **+8 EVs** in a specific stat every time the holder earns experience from a battle, regardless of what Pokemon was defeated.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Resetting Stats with Friendship Berries">

Mistakes happen, or perhaps you caught a high-level Pokemon that already has "messy" EVs from random battles. In **pokemon rejuvenation ev training**, you aren't stuck with these suboptimal stats. You can use specific **Friendship Berries** to reduce EVs by 10 points per berry.

These berries serve a dual purpose: they increase the Pokemon's happiness (Friendship) while lowering a specific EV. They are vital for fine-tuning builds or completely re-purposing a Pokemon for a different role, such as turning a Physical Tank into a Special Wall.

| **Qualot Berry** | Defense | Increases Friendship |
| **Hondew Berry** | Sp. Attack | Increases Friendship |
| **Grepa Berry** | Sp. Defense | Increases Friendship |
| **Tamato Berry** | Speed | Increases Friendship |

<Callout type="success" title="Inventory Management">
You can purchase these berries in bulk from specific vendors in larger cities like GDC or West Gearen. Always keep a stack of 20-30 of each berry in your bag so you can quickly adjust your team's stats before a major boss fight.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Optimal EV Spreads for Competitive Rejuvenation">

Deciding where to put your points is just as important as the training itself. Because Pokemon Rejuvenation features unique Field Effects that can boost certain stats or types, your **pokemon rejuvenation ev training** strategy should account for these environmental factors.

For instance, in a Trick Room field, you might actually want to avoid Speed EVs entirely and focus on HP and Attack. Conversely, in a field that boosts Electric moves, investing in Special Defense for your non-Ground types could be the difference between a sweep and a wipe.
