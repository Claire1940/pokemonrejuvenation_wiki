<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Core Mechanics of Field Effects">

In the complex world of Aevium, the environment plays a more significant role than in any traditional region. **Pokemon rejuvenation field effects** expand upon the standard terrain mechanics found in the main series, creating a dynamic system where the very ground you stand on can dictate the tide of battle. Unlike standard Terrains, these effects are often permanent unless overwritten or specifically terminated through move interactions.

Understanding how these fields interact is the difference between a crushing defeat and a tactical victory. As of the v13.5 update, there are 48 distinct fields, ranging from natural landscapes like Mountains and Deserts to synthetic environments like Glitch Fields and Factory Floors.

| Mechanic | Description | Bonus/Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Active Field** | Only one field can be active at any given time. | Dictates move boosts/drops |
| **Layering** | Fields can stack on top of each other in layers. | Bottom layers reactivate later |
| **Terrain Overlay** | Standard Terrains (Grassy/Misty) overlay active fields. | Reduced effects for the terrain |
| **Transformation** | Changing a field with a damaging move. | 1.3x Base Power boost |
| **Fieldless** | Occurs when all layers are successfully terminated. | Standard battle conditions |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip: The Power of Transformation">
If you know your move will transform the field (e.g., using a Fire move on a Grassy Field to create a Burning Field), that move receives a significant 1.3x power boost. This is often the key to breaking through a Gym Leader's defensive setup.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Methods to Alter and Disable Fields">

While many fields are tied to specific locations, a skilled trainer can manipulate the environment to their advantage. Some moves have universal effects across almost all **pokemon rejuvenation field effects**, allowing you to reset the battlefield when the opponent's advantage becomes too great.

The ability to clear a field is especially vital in high-stakes battles against the 18 Gym Leaders, who almost always utilize a field that complements their specific type specialty.

| Move/Item | Interaction Type | Duration/Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Steel Roller** | Field Disabling | Disables field for 5 turns |
| **Splintered Stormshards** | Transformation | Changes field to Rocky Field (2 turns) |
| **Domain Shift** | Random Transformation | Random field based on Route (Paragon/Renegade) |
| **Standard Terrains** | Overlay | Overlays current field with reduced buffs |

<Callout type="warning" title="Domain Shift Variance">
The Intercept Z-Move "Domain Shift" is highly unpredictable. The pool of potential fields it can transform into depends on your current story path (Paragon or Renegade). For example, Renegade players might see Infernal Fields, while Paragon players see Blessed Fields.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Master Field Manipulation">

Mastering the use of **pokemon rejuvenation field effects** requires a step-by-step approach to team building and in-battle decision making. You cannot simply rely on raw power; you must use the environment as your sixth party member.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Analyze the Environment">
    Before the battle begins, identify the active field. Note which types are boosted (usually indicated by field-specific animations) and which moves might trigger a transformation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Equip Field-Altering Moves">
    Ensure at least one Pokemon in your party carries a move like Steel Roller or a Terrain-setting move (like Electric Terrain). This allows you to "overwrite" the opponent's home-field advantage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Calculate Layering Risks">
    If you use a Terrain move, remember it only overlays the field. Once the 5-8 turns of Terrain end, the original, often more dangerous field will return. Plan your knockouts within that window.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Trigger Beneficial Transformations">
    Use moves that transform the field into something that benefits your team more than the opponent's. For instance, turning a Corrosive Field into a Wasteland can strip a Poison-type leader of their passive recovery.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Strategy: The Steel Roller Reset">
Steel Roller is one of the most consistent ways to handle oppressive fields. While it requires a field to be active to work, it effectively resets the battle to a "fieldless" state for 5 turns, giving you a neutral playing field to execute your strategy.
</Callout>

</Section>
