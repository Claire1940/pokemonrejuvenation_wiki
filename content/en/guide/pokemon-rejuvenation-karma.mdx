<Section icon={Zap} title="Understanding the Pokemon Rejuvenation Karma System">

In the complex narrative of Aevium, your actions carry weight far beyond simple dialogue flavor. The **Pokemon Rejuvenation karma** system is the invisible engine driving the game's branching storyline. Unlike standard RPGs where "good" and "evil" are clearly labeled, Rejuvenation often hides the impact of your choices until chapters later. In V14, this system has been refined to ensure that your path toward the Paragon or Renegade endings feels earned through consistent behavior.

<Callout type="warning" title="Irreversible Decisions">
Many Karma-altering choices occur in the first five chapters. While New Game+ allows for a reset, a standard playthrough requires careful planning from the start if you aim for the Renegade route.
</Callout>

Karma is gained by helping NPCs, showing mercy to antagonists, and prioritizing the safety of the group. Conversely, negative Karma is accumulated through selfishness, unnecessary violence, or abandoning allies in distress. This value is often checked during "Intermission" periods or major chapter climaxes to determine which version of a scene you witness.

Every major arc in V14 contains at least one "Critical Choice" that significantly shifts your Karma standing. These moments are often marked by a brief pause or a prompt that asks for confirmation. <Callout type="tip" title="Hidden Checks">
Some Karma changes are not announced. If you find an NPC asking for a small favor or a choice seems "flavor only," it likely still contributes +/- 1 to your total Karma score.
</Callout>

Beyond the binary choices, the game tracks "Relationship Points" (RP) for every major companion. While Karma determines the overall route, RP determines who stays by your side within that route. High RP can sometimes bypass a slightly lower Karma score, allowing you to stay on the Paragon path even after a few mistakes.

</Section>

<Section icon={Heart} title="Relationship Points and Ally Synergy">

| Character | Primary Way to Increase RP | Reward / Impact |

<Callout type="info" title="RP Benchmarks">
In Chapter 16, the game performs a "Loyalty Check." If your RP with a character is below a certain threshold, they may abandon you or fail to intervene during a boss battle, regardless of your Karma.
</Callout>

To maximize your RP, you should interact with your companions during "downtime" periods in hotels or hubs. These optional conversations often provide +1 or +2 RP and provide deeper lore regarding the history of Aevium and the threat of Team Xen.

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Chapter 16: Entering Paragon or Renegade Routes">

Chapter 16 is the definitive split in the V14 story. At this point, the game evaluates your total **Pokemon Rejuvenation karma** and specific flag triggers to lock you into one of the two primary paths. It is highly recommended to keep a backup save at the end of Chapter 15 before proceeding.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Karma Evaluation">
    The game checks if your Karma is above (Paragon) or below (Renegade) the threshold. Specific "cruelty flags" from the Terajuma and GDC arcs are also weighed here.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Point of No Return">
    You will encounter a final prompt in a historical location or Team Xen facility. Confirming your intent here locks the save file into the chosen route.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Route-Exclusive Locations">
    Paragon players will head toward locations focused on restoration, while Renegade players explore altered, darker versions of Aevium's map.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Boss Climax">
    Prepare for entirely different boss rosters. Renegade bosses often feature "corrupted" teams with Level 100+ caps and optimized competitive held items.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="New Game+ Benefits">
Completing the Paragon route first is recommended by the developers. This unlocks New Game+ features that make achieving the Renegade requirements easier in a second playthrough.
</Callout>

The Renegade route is significantly more difficult and is intended for veteran players. It features unique Rift Pokémon encounters and story revelations that are not present in the Paragon path. However, it is also the "Bad Ending" path, resulting in the loss of many beloved characters.

</Section>
