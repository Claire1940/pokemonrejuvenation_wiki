<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Understanding Pokemon Rejuvenation Crests Mechanics">

**Pokemon rejuvenation crests** represent a revolutionary item system within the fan-game, designed to breathe new life into Pokémon that typically struggle in competitive or high-difficulty environments. Unlike standard items like the Choice Band or Life Orb, a Crest is hard-coded to a specific species (or its regional forms). When held by the correct Pokémon, it triggers a variety of effects ranging from simple stat multipliers to completely custom mechanics that alter how the Pokémon functions in battle.

One of the most critical aspects of these items is their permanence in combat. In the difficult battles found throughout the 2026 version of the game, losing a held item can be a death sentence. However, Crests are protected by a unique "sticky" mechanic. While a move like Knock Off will still deal its increased damage, the Crest itself will never be removed from the user. Similarly, moves like Trick or Switcheroo will simply fail to activate if either Pokémon is holding a corresponding Crest.

| Mechanic | Effect in Battle | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Knock Off** | Damage is dealt, but the item is **not** removed. | Damage multiplier for Knock Off still applies. |
| **Trick / Switcheroo** | The move fails entirely if used on a Crest holder. | Prevents enemy disruption strategies. |
| **UI Indicator** | A specific Crest icon appears on the battle interface. | Helps players identify active effects immediately. |
| **Species Lock** | Only functions for the intended species. | Transforming users (like Ditto) can trigger the effect. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Stat Swap Warning">
Crests that swap stats (such as the Magcargo Crest) do so **after** all calculations for IVs, EVs, and Natures are applied. For example, if your Magcargo has high Defense EVs, those will effectively become Speed EVs when the Crest is active. However, in-battle stat boosts from moves like Harden will still apply to the original stat (Defense) rather than the swapped one.
</Callout>

The flexibility of these items is further demonstrated by the "Transform" interaction. If a Pokémon uses Transform (like Ditto or Mew) and turns into a species whose Crest it is currently holding, it will gain the full benefits of that Crest. Conversely, if a Pokémon holding its own Crest transforms into something else, the Crest's effects are immediately deactivated until it reverts to its original form.

</Section>
